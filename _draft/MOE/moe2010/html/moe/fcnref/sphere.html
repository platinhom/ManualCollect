MOE 2010.10

Water Accessible Surface Area Functions

Syntax

    area = AtomSurfaceArea [ atom_keys, water_radius, dot_density, environment ]
    area = SphereSurfaceArea [ positions, radii, dot_density ]
    [triangles, edge_flags, vertices] = TriangulateUnitSphere dot_density

Description

area = AtomSurfaceArea [ atom_keys, water_radius, dot_density, environment ]
area = SphereSurfaceArea [ positions, radii, dot_density ]

AtomSurfaceArea Returns an estimate of the exposed water-accessible surface area of the specified atoms (default = all atoms in the system) using a given water_radius to calculate the water-accessible surface (default = 1.4), and given dot_density (a non-negative integer, default = 1) to control the precision of the estimate. Additionally, atom keys which may or may not influence the calculation can be provided in the environment argument.
SphereSurfaceArea Returns an estimate of the exposed surface area of balls centered at given positions and of given radii. dot_density (a non-negative integer, default 1) controls the precision of the estimate.

The estimate is obtained by generating a number of dots on each ball surface and then calculating the proximity of the dots to dots on other balls. The dots are generated at the vertices of an icosahedron whose triangular surfaces are recursively subdivided into smaller ones. The grain of the tiling is controlled by the dot density (a larger number increases resolution): the number of vertices generated for each ball is 2 + 30 × 4dot_density. 

[triangles, edge_flags, vertices] = TriangulateUnitSphere dot_density

TriangulateUnitSphere returns a triangulation of the unit sphere in a laminated format, i.e. 

triangles = [[p1_x, p1_y, p1_z], [p2_x, p2_y, p2_z], [p3_x, p3_y, p3_z]]
edge_flags = [f1, f2, f3]
vertices = [v_x, v_y, v_z]

where p1_x, p1_y, .. v_y, v_z are flat numerical vectors. If the triangulation has n unique triangles with m unique vertices, vectors v_x, v_y, and v_z are of length m, while vectors p1_x, ... f3 are of length n.

The triangulation is generated by successive partitioning of a regular icosahedron. The number of vertices generated is equal to 2 + 30 × 4 dot_density. The number of triangles generated is equal to 60 × 4 dot_density.
