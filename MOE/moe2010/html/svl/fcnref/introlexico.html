Relational Functions

Relational operators that compare elements are classified into two types: lexicographic and element-wise.

Lexicographic operators make comparisons among only the top-level elements of their arguments and their names are marked by the suffix L. Arguments to lexicographic operators need not be conformable, and need not even be of the same data type. The comparison is made between full vectors.

Element-wise operators work on the leaf level elements of their arguments. The comparison is made between corresponding leaf values. Arguments must be conformable and of the same data type.
