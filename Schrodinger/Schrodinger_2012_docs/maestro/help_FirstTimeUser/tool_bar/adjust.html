Adjusting Structures

Maestro provides a number of tools for adjusting structures, that is, for moving atoms within a structure. General-purpose adjustments —translation of a group of atoms or rotation of a group of atoms with respect to a user-defined center— can be done with local transformations, which are described in the Local Transformations topic. These adjustments take no account of the bonding between atoms. The tools available from the Adjust button menu on the Edit toolbar,

allow you to adjust structures by changing bond lengths, bond angles, dihedral angles, and chiralities; by changing the conformation around peptides, and by selecting from a set of amino acid side-chain rotamers.

Adjustments are made by dragging with the left mouse button or using the scroll wheel. Dragging generally gives finer increments than the scroll wheel. You can use the middle and right mouse buttons to perform global rotations and translations as usual while you are doing adjustments.

The five groups of tools on this button menu are described below.

Quick Torsion tools

These tools allow you to quickly select a bond around which to perform a rotation, and view contacts and H-bonds with other atoms while you rotate.

  • Quick Torsion—Adjust a dihedral angle. When you choose this item, you can pick a bond ("B" picking cursor) in the Workspace, then drag horizontally or use the scroll wheel to change the dihedral angles around the bond. An arrow on the bond points to the fragment that is moved; to change the direction, click the bond again.

  • Display H-bonds—Display hydrogen bonds while rotating around the bond.

  • Display Contacts—Display bad and ugly contacts while rotating around the bond.

The two display options are saved as preferences and are applied whenever you use Quick Torsion.

Peptide linkage tools

The tools in this group allow you to quickly rearrange the orientation of the atoms in a peptide linkage, keeping the rest of the structure the same.

  • Rotate Peptide Plane—Rotate the plane of the peptide linkage. To select the peptide linkage, pick one of its atoms. The axis of rotation is the line between the alpha carbons on either side of the peptide linkage, and is marked with a semitransparent rectangular plane when you pick an atom in the linkage. To rotate the plane, drag horizontally or use the scroll wheel.

  • Rotate Carbonyl Plane—Rotate the plane of the carbonyl in a peptide linkage. To select the peptide linkage, pick one of its atoms. The axis of rotation is the line between the alpha carbons on one side of the carbonyl and the amide nitrogen on the other, and is marked with a semitransparent rectangular plane when you pick an atom in the linkage. To rotate the plane, drag horizontally or use the scroll wheel.

  • Convert Cis/Trans—Convert a peptide linkage from cis to trans or trans to cis. To convert the linkage, pick one of its atoms. The conversion keeps the rest of the structure intact, and tries to provide a reasonable placement of the atoms of the linkage. The plane of the linkage is maintained. The conversion is likely to produce strained geometries, so some kind of adjustment or minimization of the structure following the conversion is desirable.

Geometric adjustment tools

These tools allow you to pick distances, angles, dihedrals, or chiral atoms for adjustment. These adjustment tools are also available in the Adjust panel, which you open from the Edit menu.

The distance, angle, and dihedral adjustments function in a similar way. When you have selected Distance, Angle, or Dihedral from the button menu, you then pick two, three, or four atoms in the Workspace. Each atom is marked with a purple cube, until you pick the last atom. On the final pick, the cubes are replaced with adjustment markers.

You do not have to pick atoms that are bonded to each other. If the last two atoms that you pick are not bonded to each other, only the last atom is moved. If they are bonded, the last atom is moved with all its attachments.

To adjust the geometric parameter, drag horizontally or use the scroll wheel.

Chirality adjustments also require picking. When you have chosen Chirality from the button menu, pick the chiral atom, then the two atoms that are attached to it that will remain in place. The other two atoms with their attachments are swapped when you make the third pick. If you need to see which atoms are chiral, you can choose Chirality from the Label All button menu on the Labels toolbar.

Adjustment deletion

The Delete Adjustments item deletes the information about adjustments and removes the markers from the Workspace. It does not affect the adjustments made to the structure. The markers remain in the Workspace while you are doing adjustments; they are hidden if you move to some other task, but are redisplayed if you return to adjustments.

Rotamer selection tool

This tool allows you to select an amino-acid side-chain rotamer from a standard set of rotamers. To use this tool, first select the desired residues in the Workspace, then choose Rotamers from the button menu. The selection of the side chain rotamer is done in the Rotamers dialog box. You can also open this dialog box from the Adjust submenu of the Edit menu.
