Summary

As well as internal and bundled software and data, Prime can use certain external third-party programs and databases for sequence and homolog searches, protein family searches, and secondary structure prediction. These are described under the headings in this topic.

BLAST/PSI-BLAST, Pfam, and the PDB

These third-party databases and search facilities are described at their home pages:

BLAST/PSI-BLAST

http://www.ncbi.nih.gov/BLAST

HMMER/Pfam

http://pfam.wustl.edu

PDB

http://www.rcsb.org/pdb/

Secondary Structure Prediction: Prime and SSpro

Prime relies on secondary structure prediction for improving alignments and facilitating fold recognition. The use of multiple secondary structure prediction programs leads to more accurate alignments and fold recognition results. One secondary structure prediction program (SSpro) is bundled with Prime. However, PRIME supports one other secondary structure prediction program, PSIPRED V2.3.

Secondary Structure Prediction: PSIPRED

Prime supports use of the secondary structure prediction program PSIPRED V2.3 as a third-party program. This program may be manually installed by you, at your election, and subject to license terms, if any, applicable to it. At the third-party web site whose address is given below, you may download this third party program and install it.

The PSIPRED home page is located at:

http://bioinf.cs.ucl.ac.uk/psipred/

and contains information about the program, referrals to terms of use and/or license terms (in the README file), and a link to download the program. The link to download PSIPRED V2.3 is contained in the section of the page labeled "Overview of prediction methods available".

Instructions for installing PSIPRED for use with Prime can be found on the Instructions for Compiling and Installing Third-Party Programs web page
