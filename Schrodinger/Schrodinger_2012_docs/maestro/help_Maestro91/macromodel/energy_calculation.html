Summary

The Current Energy panel is used to perform single point energy calculations on structures in the Workspace or entries in the Project Table.

Opening the Current Energy Panel

To open the Current Energy panel:

  • Choose Applications → MacroModel → Current Energy

Using the Current Energy Panel

MacroModel panels allow you to choose structures from either the Workspace or the Project Table (see Energy Overview for details). The Current Energy panel contains four tabs. For descriptions of the controls in the first three tabs, see Potential, Constraints, and Substructure. The fourth tab, ECalc, contains the energy listing control, which is described below. Once you have finished configuring the settings on the panel, you can click Start to start the job or click Write to write the files without starting the job (see Energy Overview for details).

ECalc Tab Features

Energy listing
This option controls the extent of the energy listing. There are three options:

  • None: no energy listing, only a total energy printed to the log file
  • Minimal: a listing to the .mmo and .log files of all the total contributions from each type of force field term.
  • Complete: a complete listing of each interaction including showing the parameters used and the energy contribution.
    A "Complete" energy listing is required to use the Force Field Viewer panel available under the Tools menu. Note: the "Complete" listing can produce large output files (.mmo) for large structures.
