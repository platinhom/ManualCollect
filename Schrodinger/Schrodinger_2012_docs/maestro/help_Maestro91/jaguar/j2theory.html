Overview

Jaguar J2 theory calculations allow you to obtain accurate energies for molecules. The result of the calculation is an absolute enthalpy at 298K. These calculations are at least as accurate as G2 theory calculations.

The J2 theory calculations consist of a a B3LYP/6-31G* geometry optimization and frequency calculation, followed by single point GVB/LMP2 calculations using the cc-pvtz(-f) and cc-pvtz++ basis sets at the B3LYP-optimized geometries. These single-point calculations are used to determine a basis set correction energy. A parameterized electron pair correction energy is also added. The finite temperature effects are calculated from the B3LYP frequencies.

To calculate reaction enthalpies, you can perform J2 calculations on the reactants and the products, and add and subtract the final J2 energies, which are in the job output file.

Running J2 Calculations

To run a J2 theory calculation, choose Applications → Jaguar → J2. The Jaguar panel has only the Molecule and SCF tabs, because all other input is predefined. When you have selected the structures and made any settings, click Start.
