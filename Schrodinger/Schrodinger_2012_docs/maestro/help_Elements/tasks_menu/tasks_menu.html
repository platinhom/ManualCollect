Tasks Menu

The Tasks menu provides acces to the tasks you can run with Maestro Elements.

  • Minimize selected atoms—Minimize the energy of the selected atoms with respect to their positions, using the OPLS_2005 force field.

  • Measurements—Measure distances, angles, or dihedrals, and display them in the Workspace. The submenu has three items: Pick to Measure, which allows you to pick atoms in the Workspace; Advanced Measurements, which opens the Measurements panel for more measurement features; and Delete Measurements, for deleting measurement-related markers from the Workspace. The Pick to Measure item does the same as the toolbar button, and measures whatever type of measurement you selected from the toolbar button menu.

  • H-Bonds and Contacts—Show hydrogen bonds or contacts in the Workspace, and change settings for hydrogen bonds and contacts. Opens the H-Bonds and Contacts panel. You can also show H-bonds from the toolbar.

  • Superposition—Superimpose structures, in the Superposition panel.

  • Flexible Ligand Alignment—Align ligands with torsional rotation to find the best alignment, in the Flexible Ligand Alignment panel.

  • Ligand Interaction Diagram—View a 2D diagram of the interactions between the ligand and the receptor that are in the Workspace, in the Ligand Interaction Diagram panel.

  • Interactive Molecular Properties—

  • Empirical pKa Prediction (Epik)—Predict the pKa of molecules by a rule-based method, in the Epik panel.

  • Ab Initio pKa Prediction (Jaguar)—Predict the pKa of molecules by ab initio calculation with an empirical correction, using Jaguar.

  • Torsion Scan—Perform a rapid scan of a torsion to view its energy profile with the OPLS_2005 force field, in the Rapid Torsion Scan panel.

  • Minimization—Minimize the entire workspace structure either in the gas phase or in a selected solvent, using the OPLS_2005 force field.

  • Conformer Generation—Generate conformers of the Workspace structure, and add them to the project.

  • Ligand Tools—Perform a range of tasks on ligand-sized molecules.

    • Prepare Ligands (LigPrep)—;Prepare ligand-sized molecules for use in modeling, with LigPrep. Includes 2D→3D conversion, adjustment or expansion of ionization states, tautomers, ring conformations, stereochemistry, and minimization.

    • Shape-Based Similarity Screening—

    • Core Hopping—Substitute the core of a ligand for other cores, using alignment or shape to find similar cores, while retaining the side chains. Opens the Ligand-Based Core Hopping panel.

    • Isosteric Matching—

    • R-Group Analysis—Analyze the side chains of a set of ligands and display properties related to these side chains, using the R-Group Analysis panel.

    • ADME Prediction (QikProp)—Predict ADME properties of molecules, in the QikProp panel.

    • Ligand Filtering—Filter ligands based on their properties, in the Ligand Filtering panel.

    • Merge Duplicate Ligands—Merge properties of duplicate ligands and write the merged records to a 2D SD file, a SMILES file, or a CSV file with a SMILES string for the structure.

    • Electrostatic Potential—Calculate the electrostatic potential for the Workspace atoms, and return values as partial charges, or a surface, or both.

  • Protein Tools—Perform a range of tasks on protein molecules. The submenu has the following tools:

    • Protein Preparation Wizard—Prepare proteins for use in modeling, with the Protein Preparation Wizard.

    • Protein Structure Alignment—Align two or more protein structures, based on sequence and secondary structure in the Protein Structure Alignment panel.

    • Align Binding Sites—Align the binding sites of two or more proteins, in the Align Binding Sites panel.

    • Protein Reports—Display reports on protein properties, such as geometric deviations from ideal values, G-factors, B-factors, stereochemistry, planarity, missing atoms, in the Protein Reports panel.

    • Ramachandran Plot—Display Ramachandran plot of protein dihedrals.

    • Binding Site Characterization (SiteMap)—Find potential binding sites on a protein, with SiteMap.

    • Hydrophobic/philic Surface Mapping—Generate hydrophobic and hydrophilic surfaces for the Workspace structure.

    • Multiple Sequence Viewer—View and perform tasks on multiple protein sequences, such as alignment, analysis, and homology modeling, in the Multiple Sequence Viewer panel.

  • Design and Scoring—Design and score ligands for a receptor binding site. The submenu has the following tasks:

    • Docking and Lead Optimization—Dock a ligand to a receptor, then modify its structure (in 2D) and redock it, to optimize its docking score.

    • Ligand Designer: Rigid Binding Site (uses Glide)—Dock a ligand to a receptor, then modify its structure in the Workspace and redock it, to optimize its docking score.

    • Glide Grid Generation and Docking—Dock one or more ligands from the Project Table to a receptor.

    • Filter Docked Poses—Filter out docked poses that do not match criteria for interaction with the receptor that you specify.

  • Pharmacophore Search (Phase)—Create a pharmacophore hypothesis for a ligand, and screen structures to match this hypothesis.

  • Monitor Jobs—Monitor jobs that you have started, in the Monitor panel.
