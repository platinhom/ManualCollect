Minimizing A Structure

If you want to perform a quick minimization of a structure or part of a structure, you can do so by pressing Ctrl+M (⌘M). This action performs a simple force-field minimization of the energy with the OPLS_2005 force field on either the whole structure or on the selected atoms. The minimization stops when the RMSD between iterations is less than 0.1 Å or 1000 iterations have been performed. If you select atoms for minimization, the atoms bonded to the selection are included but constrained (150 kJ/mol force constant).

If you want more control over the parameters of a force-field minimization, including use of solvation, you can use MacroModel: choose Tasks → Minimization.

To perform a quantum-mechanical minimization, choose Tasks → Small Molecule Tasks → Quantum Mechanics → Optimization. For a QM/MM minimization of a large system such as a protein-ligand complex, choose Applications → QSite. For a semiempirical minimization, choose Tasks → Small Molecule Tasks → Quantum Mechanics → Semiempirical and ensure that Optimize geometry is selected.
