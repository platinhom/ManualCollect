Tasks Menu

  • Protein Preparation—Prepare proteins for use in modeling, with the Protein Preparation Wizard.

  • Homology Modeling—Predict protein structure from a protein sequence using information from homologous proteins. Two options are available on the submenu: a quick prediction or a more comprehensive prediction using the Prime Structure Prediction panel.

  • Antibody Modeling—Search for antibody models in a database, humanize the antibodies and view the results; and manage the database.

  • Residue Scanning—Scan the Workspace structure for possible mutation sites, and perform mutations at these sites to selected residues.

  • Cysteine Mutation—Mutate residues to cysteine or mutate cysteines to other residues, with formation and breaking of disulfide bonds.

  • Loop + Side Chain Prediction—Refine the structure of a protein, by predicting loops and side chains, and minimizing the energy, using Prime Refinement.

  • Protein-Protein Docking—Dock a protein to another protein, an antigen to an antibody, or a protein to itself to form a dimer or trimer.

  • Low Normal Mode Analysis—Calculate and visualize low normal modes of vibration.

  • Residue and Loop Mutation—Perform a single mutation or chirality inversion of a residue or replacement of a single loop in a protein.

  • Peptide Helicity—Predict the stability of the alpha helical conformation of a set of small peptides using molecular dynamics.

  • Residue Analysis—Analyze the energetic and other properties of the residues in the Workspace and present them in graphical form.

  • Aggregation Surface—Locate hydrophobic regions on the surface of a protein where aggregation could occur, and present the results on a surface.

  • Quick Minimize Workspace—Perform a quick minimization of the selected atoms (or all atoms, if none are selected) in the Workspace.

  • Minimize—Minimize the Workspace structure, in the Minimization panel, which offers many options for controlling the minimization.

  • Molecular Dynamics—Run molecular dynamics simulations on a periodic system that can include a protein, solvent, salt, and a membrane, using Desmond. The submenu offers the following choices:

    • System Setup
    • Molecular Dynamics
    • Simulated Annealing
    • Replica Exchange
    • Simulation Quality Analysis

  • Small Molecule Tasks—Perform tasks on ligand molecules, with or without a receptor. See the Small Molecule Tasks topic for a description of this submenu.

  • Scripts—Run scripts that have been installed with the distribution or downloaded from the Script Center.

  • KNIME Workflows—Install and run KNIME workflows.

  • Establish Structure Exchange with KNIME—Start a server to send structures to and receive structures from a KNIME session.

  • Advanced Tasks—Perform various advanced tasks on proteins or protein-ligand complexes.

    • Free-Energy Perturbation—Run free-energy perturbation calculations of relative ligand binding energies for mutated ligands and proteins, or of total free energies.

    • Protein-Ligand Scoring—Calculate ligand binding energy estimates using Prime MM-GBSA.

    • Protein X-Ray Refinement—Refine the X-ray structure of a protein using PrimeX.

    • Conformational Search—Search for conformations of a ligand (Bioactive Search) or for a structure of any size (Advanced Search, Monte Carlo.

    • Coordinate Scan—Calculate the energy of a structure as a function of one or two coordinates at defined values of the coordinates, and plot the energetic profile of the coordinates.

  • Force Field Viewer—View the values of the parameters of the force field used for a structure. This item requires a MacroModel Current Energy calculation, which must be set up in the Maestro interface (rather than the BioLuminate interface).

  • Job Monitor—Open the Monitor panel, to monitor the progress of jobs.
