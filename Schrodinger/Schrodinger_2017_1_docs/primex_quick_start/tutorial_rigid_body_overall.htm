Refining the Overall Position of p38 Kinase

1. In the PrimeX panel, click Rigid Bodies in the Reciprocal-Space Refinement section.

The Reciprocal-Space Refinement panel opens with the Rigid bodies tab displayed.

2. Choose Truncated Newton from the Minimizer option menu.
3. In the Select atoms to define group section, click the All button.

This selects all the atoms in the structure as the rigid body, so the entire structure will be moved rigidly. The atoms are marked in the Workspace.

4. Click Add.

A row is added to the Rigid body groups table, identifying the group and the residues that belong to the group. Since there are no residues that are not explicitly included, you do not need to select an option under Atoms not explicitly included in a group.

5. Click the Settings button.

The Job Settings dialog box opens.

6. Enter “Rigid body refinement, one group” in the Entry Title text box of the Job section.

The title is used for the result from this calculation, and appears as the entry title in the Project Table. The job is assigned a unique name, which you can customize if you want.

Note that the Incorporate option is Append new entries individually. This calculation generates a new structure. While you could overwrite the starting structure, it is often a good idea to keep structures from the various stages of refinements so that you can follow the progress of refinement and revert back to a previous structure if necessary.

7. Click Run.

The job takes about 5 minutes. After the job finishes, you can proceed to the second step of the rigid-body refinement.
