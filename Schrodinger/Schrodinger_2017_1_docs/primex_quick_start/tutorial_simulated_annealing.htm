Simulated Annealing Refinement for p38 Kinase

1. In the PrimeX panel, click Simulated Annealing in the Reciprocal-Space Refinement section.

The Simulated Annealing tab is displayed in the Reciprocal-Space Refinement panel.

2. Ensure that the text in the text box of the Select atoms to minimize section is all. If not, click the All button.
3. For Energy model, select Approximate.
4. Change the number of cooling steps to 250.

Shortening the refinement is done here in the interests of a quicker turnaround. The results from this short refinement are adequate, although a longer cooling phase might reduce the R-factors somewhat more.

5. In the General tab under X-ray term weight, select Constant weight and ensure that the value in the text box is 0.5.

The weight of the X-ray terms is primarily judged by the bond angle RMSD and bond length RMSD, which measure the deviation of the structure from expected geometry. While the targets for these values vary among crystallographers, values less than 2.5 degrees and 0.015Å, respectively, are considered reasonable. Decreasing this weight decreases the RMSD values, while only modestly impacting the R-factors. Weights of less than 0.1 are rarely necessary, but will vary depending on how the X-ray reflection data was processed before refinement. For reflection data on an absolute scale, a weight of 0.5 is usually optimal.

So far, no map has been generated. At the end of this refinement, we will generate a map.

6. In the Maps tab, for Coefficients select 2Fo-Fc.

You can leave the remaining options at their defaults.

7. Click the Settings button.

The Job Settings dialog box opens.

8. Enter “SA, cooling steps 250, otherwise default” in the Entry title text box of the Job section.
9. Click Run.

This job takes about 30 minutes on a 2 GHz processor. You can follow the R and R-free values in the Monitor panel, which you can open from the Applications menu or by clicking the Jobs button in the status bar of the main window. They are the last two numbers in each group of numbers that are displayed.

When the job finishes, the new structure is displayed in the Workspace with the map. Only a part of the map is displayed. You can change the display volume by spot-centering on an atom (middle-click the atom).
