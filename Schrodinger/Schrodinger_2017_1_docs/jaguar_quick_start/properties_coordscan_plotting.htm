Plotting the Chloromethyl Fluoromethyl Ether Potential Energy Surface

The results of a scan in two coordinates (from MacroModel as well as Jaguar) can be plotted as a contour plot in Maestro.

If you have not done the previous exercise (Running a Jaguar Coordinate Scan for Chloromethyl Fluoromethyl Ether), please do so now.

  1. Open the Plot of Two-Coordinate Scan panel.

    How do I do this?

    1. Click the Tasks button.

      A menu is displayed. To the left there should be a pane with a TASKS heading and an APPLICATIONS heading. If it is not displayed, click Browse on the menu.

    2. Under TASKS, choose Structure Analysis.

      The contents of the pane are replaced with the tasks available for Structure Analysis.

    3. Under Coordinate Scan, click Plot Two Coordinates.

  2. Click Open.

  3. Navigate to and select ether_scan.grd, and click OK.

    A contour plot of the scan is displayed. You can experiment with the settings.

    Note the symmetry in the plot. The large peak at 0 degrees in both coordinates is where the halogens are the closest. The minimum is at 60° in both coordinates, not at 180° where the halogens are furthest apart. This is because there is a noncovalent interaction between the halogen on each of the carbons and a hydrogen atom on the other carbon.
