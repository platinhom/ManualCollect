Creating Dimers and Trimers in Protein-Protein Docking

You can dock a protein to itself to create a dimer or a trimer. To do so, choose Dimer or Trimer in the Mode section of the Protein-Protein Docking panel. When you do, only one of the menus in the Protein structures section is available, and it is labeled Monomer. You can select the monomer protein in the same way as for the general case, and set up and run the job in the same way, as described in Docking a Protein to Another Protein.

The dimer and the trimer are subject to symmetry constraints: the dimer must have a twofold axis of rotation (C2 axis), and the trimer must have a threefold axis of rotation (C3 axis).
