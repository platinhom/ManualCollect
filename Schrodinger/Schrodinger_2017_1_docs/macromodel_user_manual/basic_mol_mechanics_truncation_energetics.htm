Energetics of Charges in MacroModel

Coulomb’s law states that the potential energy of two point charges separated by a distance r scales as 1/r. In contrast, the energy of a point charge interacting with a dipole scales as 1/r2 and the energy of two interacting dipoles scales as 1/r3. These physical properties determine the length scales for which truncation of the various interaction types is appropriate in order to achieve a given level of accuracy.
