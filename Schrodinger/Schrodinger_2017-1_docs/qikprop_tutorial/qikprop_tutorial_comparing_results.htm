Comparing Normal and Fast Mode Results

In this exercise, you will sort the entries in the Project Table to compare the results from fast mode to the results from normal mode.

1. In the Project Table panel, choose Fit To Header+Data from the Columns button menu on the toolbar.

2. Scroll through the Project Table and compare the fast mode results with the normal mode results.

Notice that, in fast mode, the dipole moment (dipole), dipole2/volume (dip^2/V), the ionization potential (IP), and the electron affinity (EA) are not predicted. Instead 0.0 is entered for these values. This is because, in the interest of saving time, the PM3 single point calculation is not performed. Notice also that the prediction for the free energy of solvation in octanol (QPlogPoct) differs between fast and normal processing modes because, in fast mode, the QSPR equations are refit without the dipole terms. The #metab descriptor also differs between the two modes.
