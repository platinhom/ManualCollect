Examining the Output Variations from LigPrep

1. In the Entry List, click the In column for entry 3, then control-click the In column for entry 4 (command-click on Mac).

The two structures generated from the first 2D structure are displayed in the Workspace, superimposed. Note the variations in the structures and the absence of the quaternary ammonium ion, which was removed by the desalter.

2. Choose View → Tile or press Ctrl+L (⌘L) .

The structures are displayed side-by-side in the Workspace. Each has a full complement of hydrogen atoms. The carboxylate group is unprotonated because its pKa lies outside the target pH range of 5 – 9. The pyridine is unprotonated.

3. In the Style toolbox, click the arrow next to Apply Labels and choose Chirality.

The chiral centers are labeled R or S. Two of the chiral centers are labeled R in both structures: these chiralities were preserved from the input structures. The remaining center is labeled R in one structure and S in the other. This center did not have chirality information in the input structure, and its chirality has been varied.

4. Choose View → Tile or press Ctrl+L (⌘L) .

The structures are superimposed again.

5. In the Entry List, click the Row column for entry 5, then shift-click the Row column for entry 10.

The six structures that were generated from the second 2D structure are selected and

6. Right-click in the selected entries, and choose Show in Workspace → Include Only.

The selected structures are displayed in the Workspace, superimposed. Note the variations in the structures.

7. Click the In column for entry 5 in the Project Table.

This entry is displayed, and the other entries are undisplayed, but still selected. The phenol group and the imidazole group are both protonated in this structure.

8. Press the Right Arrow key.

The next structure is displayed in the Workspace. The imidazole group is unprotonated.

9. Press the Right Arrow key again.

Note the tautomerization of the imidazole group.

10. Press the Right Arrow key three more times to display the remaining structures.

The last three structures have the phenol group deprotonated.
