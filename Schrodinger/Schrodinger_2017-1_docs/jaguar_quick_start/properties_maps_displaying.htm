Mapping the Vinyl Alcohol Electrostatic Potential to the Molecular Surface

In this exercise you will display a map of the electrostatic potential values on the molecular surface, in which the surface is colored by the electrostatic potential.

If you have not done the previous exercise (Generating Surfaces for Vinyl Alcohol), please do so now.

  1. Click the S button in the Entry List for the vinyl alcohol entry and choose Manage.

    The Manage Surfaces panel opens.

  2. Check the box in the In column for the electron density surface.

  3. Click Display Options.

    The Surface Display Options panel opens.

  4. Under Color scheme, select Mapped from volume.

  5. Select vinyl_alcohol_potential from the volume data list.

  6. From the Color ramp option menu, choose Rainbow.

  7. Click the Reset button if it is active.

  8. Click OK.

The density surface is colored by the values of the electrostatic potential at the surface. Note the negative (red) region near the oxygen, and the blue-purple (positive) region near the hydroxyl hydrogen.
