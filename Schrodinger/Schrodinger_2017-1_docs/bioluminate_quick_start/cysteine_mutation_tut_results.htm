Examining the 1vhu Cysteine Mutation Results

In this exercise, you will examine the best scoring cysteine mutation results.

1. In the Results tab, scroll the table to the right and click on the heading of the Weighted Score column.

The residue pairs are sorted by this score. Four of the mutations have scores of around 400, while the other two have scores around 10000. The large scores have an offset added because they fail to meet one of the cutoff criteria on the change in interaction energy (< 5.0) or strain energy (< 35.0).

2. Scroll back to the left.

Note that the original cysteine pair (A:111-A-154) is ranked at the top.

3. Click the A:111-A:154 row.

The residue pair is displayed in the Workspace, colored by element and represented in ball-and-stick. The original protein is also present, colored gray.

All of the mutated structures are included in the project. The mutation is identified by the res pair property. You can view the properties in the Project Table, which you open by pressing Ctrl+T (⌘T) in the main window.
