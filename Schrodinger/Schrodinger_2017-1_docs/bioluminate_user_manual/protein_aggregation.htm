Predicting Aggregation Regions

Protein aggregation often occurs via hydrophobic regions. You can locate and analyze these regions using the Aggregation Surface panel, which you open by choosing Tasks → Biologics → Aggregation Surface in the Task Tool. Aggregation regions are defined by identifying clusters of exposed hydrophobic residues, and creating a molecular surface that is colored red in the regions near these residues.

Once you have located potential aggregation regions on a protein, you might want to mutate residues in these regions to reduce the tendency to aggregate.
