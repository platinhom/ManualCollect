Surface Complementarity from Residue Scanning

The surface complementarity is calculated using the method of Lawrence and Colman [10], which is outlined here. The complementarity at any point on the molecular surface (Connolly surface [11]) of one protein (A) is determined by finding the nearest point on the molecular surface of the other protein (B), and comparing the vectors normal to the two surfaces at those points, with a weighting function involving the distance between the points.
