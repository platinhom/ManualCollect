Using a Ligand Similarity Search to Find Factor Xa Binders

In this exercise, we will look at ligands similar to the 1F0R ligand that binds to Factor Xa. 1F0R's ligand is a cyclic amidine inhibitor for Factor Xa. We know that this class had some of the most potent binders known to Factor Xa, but that some of the properties of amidines make drug development challenging. We'd like to find alternative moieties to interact with the S1 pocket.

  1. In the history panel, click the New Search button to begin a new query.

    We want to do a ligand similarity search using the ligand for 1F0R. Instead of drawing the structure out in the sketcher, we will import the structure from a SMILES string.

  2. Choose the SMILES String option from the Import from menu at the bottom of the search form.


  3. In the Paste SMILES dialog, copy and paste the following SMILES string:

    c1cc2c(cc(s2)S(=O)(=O)N[C@H]3CCN(C3=O)Cc4ccc5ccnc(c5c4)N)nc1

    and click OK.



    The 1F0R ligand is displayed in the sketcher.

  4. In the panel to the left of the search form, check the Ligand similarity checkbox.

    We will leave the cutoff at the default of 0.4.

  5. We'll also pull in the binding affinity data available for these results. To do this, check all of the Binding Affinity checkboxes in the Ligand Features section of the search form.

  6. Name your search "1F0R ligand similarity" and click Start Search.

    Your query should take under a minute to finish running.

  7. Open the query results by clicking the View Results button in the history panel.

  8. First, we want to sort the results in order of descending ligand similarity. Click on the header of the Similarity column in the results table.

    We see that 1F0R is at the top of the list with a similarity of 1.0.

  9. Scroll down the list and examine the compounds.

    A number of the most similar compounds are Factor Xa inhibitors that show different interaction motifs with the protein. For example, we see that 1F0S is a protonated pyridine. 2J38 shows the halogen motif that enabled researchers to bypass the amides for better PK properties.  In this way, we are able to quickly identify potential core hops/bioisosteres among data that has already been deposited.

    The list shows other protein structures that are structurally related to Factor Xa, like Trypsin (1F0T) and thrombin (4AYV), that are known common off-target problems for Factor Xa binders.
