Finding Hinge-Binding Ligand Indoles

For commonly-defined interactions and annotations such as hydrogen bonds, pi-pi interactions, the regions of kinase binding sites, and solvent accessibility, the PLDB offers annotation search. Let's use these search tools to find ligand indoles forming a hydrogen bond to the hinge region of a kinase.

Creating and Running the Query

  1. In ligand mode, draw a ligand indole and a protein residue blob in the sketcher.

  2. Add an explicit hydrogen to the ligand nitrogen. This explicit hydrogen is a necessary part of the query because the PLDB defines hydrogen bonds between the donor hydrogen and the acceptor heavy atom.

  3. Click Add Pairwise Interaction in the toolbar at the bottom of the search form.


    Then click on the hydrogen atom, and then the Any residue blob.



  4. In the dialog that opens, expand the Common Interactions group, select Hydrogen Bond, and click Ok.

  5. Right-click on the Any residue blob and click Set annotation.

  6. Expand the Kinase Features group in the dialog that opens, choose Hinge Region, and click Ok.

    You can mouse over the interactions to see a tooltip explaining the kind of interaction you have specified:

  7. Set the Search Name to "indole tutorial", and click the Start Search button to run the query.

    This query should finish running within a few minutes.

Viewing the Results

  1. Click the View Results button in the history panel to look at the results for the query.


    In this table the only additional column you see, N1, is the type of hinge residue that hydrogen bonds to the ligand indole.

  2. Click the Show Plots button to generate a bar chart showing the value of N1 across your results set.


    We can see that this interaction is most frequently made with methionine.

  3. Click on the LEU bar to bring all results where N1 is leucine into the results table.

  4. Shift-click to select all of the results in the results table, and then click Import Selected Results to Project Table to pull the results into Maestro.

    When you view a result from this query in Maestro, you will see the structural annotations that were originally queried on, the hydrogen bond and the hinge region in this case, mapped onto the 3D structure.


    Here, the hydrogen bond is shown as a dashed magenta line between the ligand hydrogen and the protein oxygen. The hinge region is highlighted in pink on the structure as well as in the sequence viewer at the bottom of the screen.

  5. Shift-click multiple rows in the Maestro Entry List to include multiple results in the Maestro Workspace.

  6. Now let's align all of the structures in the Maestro Workspace using the PLDB's structural kinase domain alignment. To do this, choose Align by→ Kinase domain in the Protein-Ligand Database panel.

    The structures are aligned by the PLDB's Kinase structural alignment. The hinge domains and hydrogen bonds that were queried on remain highlighted.

  7. Click the Ribbons button on the Workspace Configuration toolbar to hide the ribbons.

    This helps to better visualize and understand the similarity of the ligands.
