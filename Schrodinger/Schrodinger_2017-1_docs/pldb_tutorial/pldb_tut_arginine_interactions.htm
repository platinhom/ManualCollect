Searching for Arginine-Ligand Interactions

Create a search to retrieve all complexes where the ligand meets all of the following criteria: 1.) it has two hydrogen bonds with an arginine amino acid side chain, 2.) it has a π system roughly in the same plane defined by the arginine hydrogen bonds, and 3.) Its π system has a π-π stacking interaction with the protein

  1. Add an Arg residue blob to the sketcher.

  2. Right-click the residue blob and choose Expand Residue.

    The residue blob is expanded into its constituent atoms.

  3. Erase the unnecessary atoms and add explicit hydrogens to the guanidinium group.


  4. In ligand mode, draw two nitrogen atoms in the sketcher.

  5. Right-click on one of the nitrogen atoms and choose Set allowed atoms.

  6. Enter N,O in the Edit Atom dialog box and click OK.

  7. Do the same for the other ligand nitrogen atom.

  8. Click the Add Pairwise Interaction button at the bottom of the sketcher.

  9. Click the N,O ligand atom and then the protein hydrogen atom.

  10. From the Common Interactions group, select Hydrogen Bond and click Ok.

  11. Do this again for the second N,O and H.

  12. Draw a ligand carbon atom in the sketcher.

  13. Right-click the carbon atom and select Set as any aromatic ring wildcard.

  14. Click the Add Structural Annotation button on the toolbar at the bottom of the sketcher. Then draw a ring around the aromatic ring wildcard.


  15. Select Pi-Pi Interaction from the Common Interactions section in the Structural Annotation dialog, and click Ok.

  16. The next step is to specify that the plane of the guanidinium group is approximately parallel to the plane of the ligand aromatic ring, and apply a reasonable distance constraint.

    To do this, click the plane tool and lasso the guanidinium group. Use the plane tool again to lasso the any aromatic ligand wildcard.

    Then choose the angle tool. Click on the guanidinium plane and then the plane of the ligand wildcard. Enter a constraint of less than 10 degrees.

    Choose the centroid tool and lasso the any aromatic ligand wildcard. Then use the distance tool to impose a distance constraint between 5 and 8Å between the centroid of the ligand wildcard and the central carbon of the guanidinium group.

  17. Complete the search by requiring that the Guanidinium group and ligand aromatic ring are in the same plane (edge-to-edge, not stacked). We will define a vector between their two center points, and require that the angle between that vector is nearly perpendicular to the plane normal of the ligand ring.

    Use the vector tool to select the centroid of the ligand ring and the central carbon of the guanidinium group.

    Then use the angle tool to select the vector and the plane of the ligand ring. Impose a constraint between 80 and 90°.


  18. Set the Search Name to "arg-ligand interactions" and start the search. There are about 18 matches in the PLDB for this query. Here is an example result (PDB ID 3A9H), shown in Maestro.
