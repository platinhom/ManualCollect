Finding Macrocycles

In this exercise we will search for macrocycles of 25 atoms or larger.

  1. In the history panel, click the New Search button to begin a new query. We will be using the sketcher on the right side of the search form for this exercise.

  2. Select Ligand from the Draw drop-down menu at the bottom of the sketcher to enter ligand drawing mode.

    When you are in ligand drawing mode, substructures you draw in the sketcher are drawn in black.

  3. Add a single carbon atom to the sketcher.

  4. Right-click on the carbon atom and choose Ring Membership → Ring size → Custom ring size.

  5. In the Ring size dialog, filter on structures that have rings with 25 to 100 atoms, and click OK.

  6. Set "macrocycles" as the title for your query in the Search Name field, and click the Start Search button to begin the search.

  7. Once we see the query has started running, we would like to share our search with one of our colleagues. We can do this from the history panel (and also from the results panel) by clicking the share button.

    This button opens the share search dialog.

    Note: Users must be given permissions in order to share queries within a group or organization. If you try to share your query and receive a "No users or projects available for sharing" error, please contact PLDB server administrators to obtain sharing permissions within your group.

  8. Type to select users or projects to share your query with, or select users or projects from the menu in the dialog.


  9. Press enter to select a user or project to share the query with.

    The user or project is added to the share search dialog box.

    To remove a user or project from the list, click the orange button corresponding to the entity you wish to remove.

  10. Click the Share button to share your query.

    If your query is successfully shared, a confirmation message is displayed. Your query indicates that it has been shared in the My Searches tab of the history panel, as well as the Shared Searches tab.
