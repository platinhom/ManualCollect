Align Atoms Panel

This panel allows you to align the view of a molecule on one of the Cartesian axes or planes, and to set the coordinates of the molecule to this alignment.

Align Atoms Panel Features

  • Align two atoms to an axis options
  • Align three or more atoms to a plane tools
  • Update Coordinates button
Align two atoms to an axis options

The three options allow you to select the axis that you want to align to. The x axis is the horizontal axis, the y axis is the vertical axis, and the z axis is the axis that projects into the screen.

To perform the alignment to the chosen axis, select Pick atoms, then pick the two atoms that you want to align to the axis. The structure is rotated through the smallest possible angle to perform the alignment.

Align three or more atoms to a plane tools

The three options define the plane to which the Workspace structure will be aligned. The xy plane is the plane of the screen; the xz and the yz planes are the horizontal and vertical planes perpendicular to the screen.

To perform the alignment to the chosen plane, use the picking tools in the Pick atoms to align section to pick three atoms. When the third atom is picked and a check is performed to ensure that the plane is uniquely defined (i.e. the atoms are not collinear), a blue-green plane is displayed in the Workspace, and the Align button becomes available. Click this button to perform the alignment. The structure is rotated through the minimum angle required to bring the selected atoms into alignment with the chosen plane.

Update Coordinates button

Clicking this button changes the coordinates of the Workspace structure to those of the view. In other words, the structure itself is aligned on the axis or plane, and not just the view of the structure.
