General Guidelines for Convergence of MacroModel Calculations

It is as important to fully explore the potential energy surface as it is to describe that surface correctly by means of a high-quality force field. We use the term “convergence” to describe this issue. A common problem in modeling is obtaining unconverged results: the calculation gives a significantly different answer if the calculation continues or is repeated from different initial conditions. The term “convergence” has somewhat different implications, depending on the nature of the procedure that is being carried out, and for each type of energy calculation, there are different convergence issues.
