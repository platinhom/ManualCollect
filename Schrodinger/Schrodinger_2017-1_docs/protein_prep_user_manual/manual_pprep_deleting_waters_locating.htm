Locating Structural Waters in a Protein

Structural waters can be located by displaying atoms within a certain distance of the ligand. The procedure below displays water molecules as balls, which makes them easier to identify in the Workspace.

  1. Click the Select ligands (only) button under Quick Select on the toolbar.

    The ligand should be clearly distinguishable. The water molecules are represented only by the oxygen atoms, because hydrogens are not included.

  2. Click Expand and choose one of the Within N Å radius options.

    The ligand plus all atoms (including water oxygens) within the chosen distance of the ligand are now selected.

  3. Click the Remove from selection button.

  4. Click all three buttons under Quick Select.

    Only the structural waters should be selected. To check this, click the Display selected atoms only button in the Style toolbox.

    If there are any non-water atoms displayed, pick them in the Workspace to remove them from the selection.

  5. In the Styletoolbox, click the ball-and-stick button.

    The water oxygens (assuming no hydrogens have been added) are displayed as balls.

  6. Click the Remove from selection button again to return to normal picking mode.
