Manually Adjusting Protonation and Tautomerization

You should look for inconsistencies in hydrogen bonding to see whether a misprotonation of the ligand or the protein might have left two acceptor atoms close to one another without an intervening hydrogen bond. One or more residues may need to be modified to resolve such an acceptor-acceptor or donor-donor clash. In addition, you should check for the correct protonation and tautomerization of HIS residues, and for protonation of ASP, GLU, LYS, and ARG.

To protonate a neutral atom:

1. Change the formal charge on the atom to the correct value.
2. Click on the atom that you want to protonate.
3. Click the Add hydrogens button in the 3d Buillder panel.

4. Check that the formal charge is now correct by labeling the atoms, as described in Display Tasks for Correcting Protein Structures.

To deprotonate an atom:

1. Right-click on the hydrogen you want to remove and choose Delete.
2. Click on the atom to which the hydrogen was attached.
3. Click the Decrement formal charge button in the 3d Builder panel.

4. Check that the formal charge is now correct by labeling the atoms, as described in Display Tasks for Correcting Protein Structures.

To change the tautomerization:

1. Delete the hydrogen atom that needs to be moved (right-click and choose Delete).
2. Change the bond orders between the atoms to reflect the new tautomer.
3. Change any formal charges to reflect the new tautomer.
4. Add the hydrogen to its new location (pick the atom and use the Add hydrogens button).
