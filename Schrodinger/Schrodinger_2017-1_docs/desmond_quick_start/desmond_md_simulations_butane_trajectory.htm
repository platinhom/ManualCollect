Viewing the Butane Trajectory

The next part of this exercise is to view the trajectory for just the butane molecule. The display is set up first, then the trajectory viewer is opened from the Project Table panel.

  1. Click the Choose item to select button on the Select toolbar and choose Waters.

  2. In the Style toolbox click the Undisplay atoms button.

  3. Click the Set selection scope button and choose Molecules.

  4. Pick an atom in the butane molecule.

  5. In the Style toolbox click the Ball & Stick button.

  6. In the Entry List panel, click the T button for the butane simulation output entry.

    There should be only one such button. The Trajectory panel opens.

  7. In the Frame control section, enter 1 in the Step text box.

  8. Adjust the Speed slider to about the middle.

  9. In the Display section, deselect Show simulation box and Show axes.

  10. Select Superimpose on frame, and enter 1 in the text box.

    By default, the first molecule (the solute) is selected to superimpose: the ASL expression reads mol.num 1. Here, we will choose two atoms.

  11. In the Pick atoms for frame positioning section, click the Clear button.

  12. Ensure that Pick is selected, and pick the second and third carbon of the butane molecule.

  13. Click the Play forward button.

You should see the butane molecule switch to the gauche and to the cis conformation during the course of the trajectory. If the play is too fast or too slow, adjust the Speed slider.
