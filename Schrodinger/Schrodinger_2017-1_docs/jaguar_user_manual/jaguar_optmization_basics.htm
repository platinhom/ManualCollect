Jaguar Geometry Optimization: The Basics

To perform a geometry optimization, you need a guess at the geometry and the direction in which to search, a set of coordinates to optimize, and some criteria for when the optimization is complete. The search direction is obtained from the gradient of the energy and the initial Hessian. The general settings for geometry optimizations are in the Optimization tab of the Jaguar panel. This tab is present for all of the optimization and scan tasks except Rigid Coordinate Scan, for which no optimization takes place. Four of the tasks have tabs in which settings are made that are specific to the task. These tabs—Transition State, Scan, and IRC—are described in later sections.
