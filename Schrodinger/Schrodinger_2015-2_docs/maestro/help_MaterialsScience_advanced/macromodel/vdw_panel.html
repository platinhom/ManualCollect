Summary

The Van der Waals panel is used to display all the van der Waals (VDW) interactions for a given energy calculation.

Opening the Force Field Viewer's Van der Waals Panel

To open the Force Field Viewer's Van der Waals panel, you can:

  • Click Van der Waals in the Force Field Viewer panel.

Using the Force Field Viewer's Van der Waals Panel

The Van der Waals List on the left side of the Van der Waals panel displays all the van der Waals interactions present the selected .mmo file, in the order they were read from the file. To select a particular interaction, highlight it in the list by clicking on it with the left mouse button, or pick the atoms defining it from the Workspace structure. The text boxes on the panel will update to display data relevant to the selected torsion.

Van der Waals Interactions List:

All the interactions in a given .mmo file are displayed in the Van der Waals Interactions List located on the left side of the "Van der Waals" panel. The text box in which the list appears is a read-only field.

Find by Picking:

To locate a particular van der Waals interaction in the list, enable the "Find by Picking" toggle button and click on the two atoms in the structure that define the torsion. Once the second atom has been picked (and assuming an entry for the atoms can be found), the interaction corresponding to those two atoms will be selected in the list.

Sort by Energy:

By default, the interactions that are displayed in the list appear in the order in which they were read from the .mmo file. However, the list may be sorted according to energy by enabling the "Sort By Energy" toggle button. The interaction with the lowest (i.e. most negative) energy will then be displayed at the beginning of the list and the one with the highest energy (i.e. most positive) will be displayed at the end.

Show:

When Maestro first reads an .mmo file, all of the van der Waals interactions are displayed on the "Van der Waals" panel. To limit the displayed interactions based on quality, select the "High Quality," "Medium Quality," or "Low Quality" menu option from the "Show:" menu. Limiting the display to only the low quality parameters is a useful way to identify those that might be generalized or that might not be suitable for calculations in which a high degree of accuracy is desired. Clicking "All Parameters" option redisplays all torsions.

Show Force Field:

Clicking on the "Show Force Field" button opens the "FField" panel, which displays the currently selected .mmo file in its entirety. When the panel is opened, a text line will be selected. This line corresponds to the van der Waals interaction that is currently selected in the Van der Waals Interactions List.

VDW Energy:

The value that appears in this text field represents the contribution to the total molecular mechanics energy arising from the atom pair selected in the Van der Waals Interactions List.

Distance:

The number in the "Distance:" text field is the interatomic distance of the selected atom pair. Note that only atoms that are more than two bonds and less than the cutoff distance apart are considered.

Constant 1:

The value in this field is derived differently for different force fields. Generally, this constant pertains to the epsilon term of the van der Waals equation.

Constant 2:

The value in this field is derived differently for different force fields. Generally, this constant pertains to the van der Waals radii of the atoms in the selected pair.
