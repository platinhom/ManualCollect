Summary

Analyze the density of a solid as a function of temperature to determine the coefficient of thermal expansion and the glass transition temperature. The densities are obtained from MD simulations at a set of temperatures in the NPT ensemble.

Opening the Thermophysical Properties Analysis Panel

To open the Thermophysical Properties Analysis panel, you can:

  • Choose Tasks → Thermophysical Properties → Analyze Results in the main window.

  • Choose Applications → Materials Science → Thermophysical Properties → Analyze Results in the main window.

Thermophysical Properties Analysis Panel Features

  • Load Data from Workspace button
  • Plot area
  • Remove Selected Points from Fits button
  • Restore All Points button
  • Equation, R2 and property boxes
  • Store CTE and Tg button
Load Data from Workspace button

Load the data from the Workspace structure for analysis. The structure displayed in the Workspace must be the output from a thermophysical properties calculation.

Plot area

This area displays the plot of the specific volume against the temperature, and displays the regression lines for the low and high temperature regions that are used to determine the coefficient of thermal expansion and the glass transition temperature. The toolbar is a standard toolbar with controls for managing the plot, and is described in the Plot Toolbar topic.

Points can be selected in the plot area and removed from the fits by clicking Remove Selected Points from Fits.

The points used for the two fits are indicated by the two colored regions: green for the low-temperature (glassy) region and blue for the high-temperature (rubbery) region. You can drag the boundaries of these regions to change the sets of points included in each fit. The data for the fits are updated in the panel when you make the changes.

Remove Selected Points from Fits button

Click this button after selecting one or more points on the plot to remove them from the fits for the properties.

Restore All Points button

Restore all points to the plot to be used for fitting.

Equation, R2 and property boxes

These boxes display the equation of the lines of best fit and the squared correlation coefficient R2 for the glassy (low T) and rubbery (high T) regions, and the derived coefficient of thermal expansion and the glass transition temperature. They are updated as you remove points from the plot or restore points to the plot.

Store CTE and Tg button

Save the coefficient of thermal expansion and the glass transition temperature as properties to the structure that was loaded. This button allows you to save the properties when you are satisfied with the fits to the data. It is not done automatically.
